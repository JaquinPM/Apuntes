## Subspaces

_Definition 1.33: Subspace_.
A subset $U \subseteq V$ is called a subspace of $V$ if $U$ is also a vector space with the same additive identity, addition, and scalar multiplication as on $рқ‘ү$.

_Theorem 1.34: Conditions for a subspace_.
A subset $рқ‘Ҳ$ of $рқ‘ү$ is a subspace of $рқ‘ү$ if and only if $рқ‘Ҳ$ satisfies the following three conditions.
- additive identity: $0 \in рқ‘Ҳ$.
-  closed under addition: $рқ‘ў, рқ‘ӨвҲҲрқ‘Ҳ \implies рқ‘ў+ рқ‘ӨвҲҲрқ‘Ҳ$.
- closed under scalar multiplication: $рқ‘ҺвҲҲрқҗ… \wedge рқ‘ўвҲҲрқ‘Ҳ \implies рқ‘Һрқ‘ўвҲҲрқ‘Ҳ$.

_Definition 1.36: Sum of subspaces_.
Suppose $рқ‘ү_1, вҖҰ , рқ‘ү_рқ‘ҡ$ are subspaces of $рқ‘ү$. The sum of $рқ‘ү_1, вҖҰ , рқ‘ү_m$, denoted by
$рқ‘ү_1 + вӢҜ+ рқ‘ү_рқ‘ҡ$, is the set of all possible sums of elements of $рқ‘ү_1, вҖҰ , рқ‘ү_рқ‘ҡ$. More
precisely,
$$рқ‘ү_1 + вӢҜ+ рқ‘ү_рқ‘ҡ= \{рқ‘Ј_1 + вӢҜ+ v_рқ‘ҡвҲ¶v_1 вҲҲv_1, вҖҰ , v_рқ‘ҡ вҲҲ V_рқ‘ҡ \}.$$

_Theorem 1.40: Sum of subspaces is the smallest containing subspace_.
Suppose $рқ‘ү_1, вҖҰ , рқ‘ү_рқ‘ҡ$ are subspaces of $рқ‘ү$. Then $рқ‘ү_1 + вӢҜ+ рқ‘ү_рқ‘ҡ$ is the smallest
subspace of $рқ‘ү$ containing $рқ‘ү_1, вҖҰ , рқ‘ү_рқ‘ҡ$.

_Theorem 1.45: Conditions for a direct sum_
Suppose $рқ‘ү_1, вҖҰ , рқ‘ү_рқ‘ҡ$ are subspaces of $рқ‘ү$. Then $рқ‘ү_1 + вӢҜ+ рқ‘ү_рқ‘ҡ$ is a direct sum if
and only if the only way to write $0$ as a sum $рқ‘Ј_1 + вӢҜ+ рқ‘Ј_рқ‘ҡ$, where each $рқ‘Ј_рқ‘ҳвҲҲрқ‘ү_рқ‘ҳ$, is by taking each $рқ‘Ј_рқ‘ҳ$ equal to $0$.

_Theorem 1.46: Direct sum of two subspaces._
Suppose $U$ and $W$ are subspaces of $V$. Then
$$U+W \text { is a direct sum } \iff U \cap W = \{0\}$$
