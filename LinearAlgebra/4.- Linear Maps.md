_Definition 3.1 Linear map_

A linear map from $V$ to $W$ is a function $T:V \to W$ with the following properties
- Additivity: $T(u+v) = Tu + Tv$ 
- Homogeneity: $T(\lambda v) = \lambda(Tv)\quad \forall \lambda \in F$

_3.4 Linear Map Lemma_

Suppose $v_1,\dots, v_n$ is a basis of $V$ and $w_{1},\dots,w_{n}\in W$. Then there exists a unique linear map $T: V \to W$ such that
$$Tv_{k} = w_{k}$$
for each $k=1,...,n$.

_3.7 Definition: Product of linear maps_

If $T\in \mathcal{L}(U,W)$ and $S\in \mathcal(V,W)$, then the product $ST\in \mathcal{L}(U,W)$ is defined by $$(ST)(u)=S(Tu)$$ for all $u\in U$.

_Algebraic properties of products of linear maps_

- Associativity: $(T_{1}T_{2})T_{3}= T_{1}(T_{2}T_{3})$ 
- Identity: $TI= IT$ whenever $T\in \mathcal{L}(V,W)$: here the first $I$ is the identity operator on $V$, and the second $I$ is the identity operator on $W$
- Distributive properties: 
	 $(S_{1}+ S_{2})T = S_{1}T + S_{2}T \wedge S(T_{1}+T_2) = ST_{1}+ST_{2},$
	 $T,T_{1}, T_{2} \in \mathcal{L}(U,V) \wedge S,S_{1},S_{2}\in \mathcal{L}(V,W)$
