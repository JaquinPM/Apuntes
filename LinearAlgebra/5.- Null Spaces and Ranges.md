_3.11 Definition: Null Space, null T_
For $T\in \mathcal{L}(V,W)$ the null space of $T$, denoted by $null \space T$, is the subset of $V$ consisting of those vectors that $T$ maps to $0$.
$$null \space T = \{v \in V : Tv=0\}$$

_3.13 Theorem. The Null Space is a subspace_
Suppose $T\in \mathcal{L}(U,V)$, Then $null \space T$ is a subspace of $V$.

_3.15 Theorem. Injectivity $\iff$ null space equals {0}_
Let $T\in \mathcal{L}(U,V)$. Then $T$ is injective if and only if $null \space T = \{0\}$.

_3.18 Theorem. The Range is a subspace_
Suppose $T\in \mathcal{L}(V,W)$, Then $range \space T$ is a subspace of $W$.

_3.22 Theorem. Fundamental theorem of linear maps_
Suppose $V$ is finite-dimensional and $T\in \mathcal{L}(V,W)$. Then $range \space  T$ is finite dimensional and 
$$dim \space V = dim \space null \space T + dim\space range $$
_3.22 Theorem. Linear map to a lower-dimensional space is not injective._
Suppose $V$ and $W$ are finite-dimensional vector spaces such that $dim \space V > dim \space W$. Then no linear map from $V$ to $W$ is injective.

_2.24 Theorem Linear map to a higher-dimensional space is not surjective_
Suppose $V$ and $W$ are finite-dimensional vector spaces such that $dim \space V < dim \space W.$ Then no linear map from $V$ to $W$ is surjective.

_3.26 Theorem. Homogeneous system of linear equations_
A homogeneous system of linear equations with more variables than equations has nonzero solutions.
