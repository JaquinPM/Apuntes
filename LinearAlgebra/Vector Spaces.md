_Definition 1.20 Vector space_: A vector space is a set $V$ along with an addition on $V$ and a scalar multiplication on $V$ such that the following properties hold.
- Commutativity: $u+v = v + u, \forall v \in V$
- Associativity: $(u+v) + w = u + (v+w)$ and $(ab)v = a(bv), \space \forall  u,v,w \in V \wedge \forall a,b \in F$
- Additive identity: $\exists 0 \in V \space | \space v + 0 = v, \quad \forall v \in V$
- Additive inverse: $\forall v \in V, \exists w \in V \space | \space v+w = 0$
- Multiplicative identity: $1v = v \quad \forall v \in V$
- Distributive properties: $a(u+v) = au + av \wedge (a+b)v =av +bv \quad \forall a,b \in F \wedge \forall u,v \in V$
