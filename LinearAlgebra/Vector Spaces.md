## Vector Spaces

_Definition 1.20: Vector space_ 
A vector space is a set $V$ along with an addition on $V$ and a scalar multiplication on $V$ such that the following properties hold.
- Commutativity: $u+v = v + u, \forall v \in V$
- Associativity: $(u+v) + w = u + (v+w)$ and $(ab)v = a(bv), \space \forall  u,v,w \in V \wedge \forall a,b \in F$
- Additive identity: $\exists 0 \in V \space | \space v + 0 = v, \quad \forall v \in V$
- Additive inverse: $\forall v \in V, \exists w \in V \space | \space v+w = 0$
- Multiplicative identity: $1v = v \quad \forall v \in V$
- Distributive properties: $a(u+v) = au + av \wedge (a+b)v =av +bv \quad \forall a,b \in F \wedge \forall u,v \in V$
_Notation 1.24_: $F^S$  
- If $S$ is a set, then $F^S$ denotes the set of functions from $S$ to $F$.
- For $f,g \in F^S$, the $sum f+g\in F^S$ is the function defined by
 $$(f+g)(x) = f(x) + g(x) \quad \forall x\in S$$
- For $\lambda \in F$ and $f\in F^S$, the product $\lambda f \in F^S$ is the function defined by
$$(\lambda f)(x) = \lambda f(x) \quad\forall x\in S$$

_Theorem 1.26: Unique additive identity_
A vector space has a unique additive identity

_Theorem 1.27: Unique additive inverse_
Every element in a vector space has a unique additive inverse

_Theorem 1.30: The number 0 times a vector_
$0v = 0 \space \forall v\in V$
